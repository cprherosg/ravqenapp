# Ravqen Coaching Framework

## Core Principle
Ravqen should prescribe training the way an experienced coach would:

- the exercise target drives the station
- time acts as a cap or pacing guardrail, not the prescription itself
- warm-up prepares the exact demands of the session
- cooldown unloads the tissues and energy systems stressed in the session

## Prescription Model
Every exercise belongs to one of these prescription types:

- `reps`
- `hold`
- `time`
- `calories`
- `distance`
- `steps`

For each exercise, Ravqen should carry:

- target
- prescription type
- work cap in seconds
- rest in seconds
- cycles
- coaching note

## Coaching Rules By Program

### Complimentary
- Goal: simple, confidence-building full-body resistance work
- Warm-up: low-complexity machine/dumbbell prep and trunk activation
- Main work: 8-14 rep machine or dumbbell sets, moderate rest, repeatable quality
- Cooldown: low-load flush work and controlled breathing
- Progression: add small load or 1-2 reps before adding complexity

### Strength
- Goal: force production and progressive overload
- Warm-up: brace, squat, hinge, and unilateral prep
- Main work: lower reps, high quality, long rest, strong intent
- Cooldown: unload the main lower-body and trunk tissues
- Progression: load and quality first, density second

### Pump
- Goal: local muscular fatigue and metabolic stress
- Warm-up: blood-flow and tissue prep
- Main work: moderate reps, moderate loads, shorter but usable rest
- Cooldown: easy movement and breathing reset
- Progression: volume and density before big load jumps

### Hyper
- Goal: hypertrophy through tension, control, and unilateral stability
- Warm-up: prepare ranges and single-leg control
- Main work: 8-14 reps, slower control, enough rest to repeat cleanly
- Cooldown: bring tone down and restore movement quality
- Progression: more tension, better control, then more load

### H.I.R.T
- Goal: resistance-led training under conditioning pressure
- Warm-up: prepare both movement and breathing demand
- Main work: resistance stations with shorter recovery
- Cooldown: breathing downshift first, mobility second
- Progression: trim recovery carefully without losing movement quality

### Power
- Goal: fast force expression and crisp explosiveness
- Warm-up: neural readiness, stiffness, and landing quality
- Main work: very short work bouts, longer recovery, no grinding
- Cooldown: settle the nervous system and unload impact tissues
- Progression: only increase if bar speed and movement intent stay high

### Hyrox
- Goal: run-station efficiency and race-specific pacing
- Warm-up: running mechanics, bracing, and station rehearsal
- Main work: race-style distances and station targets, time as a pacing cap
- Cooldown: walking breath-down and lower-body recovery
- Progression: better pacing and smoother transitions before more volume

### Cardio Summit
- Goal: aerobic durability and threshold control
- Warm-up: raise temperature and rhythm before long intervals
- Main work: longer sustainable aerobic efforts
- Cooldown: breathing downshift and lower-leg/hip recovery
- Progression: longer sustainable output, not random sprinting

### Cardio U
- Goal: pacing skill and repeatability
- Warm-up: rhythm-focused prep
- Main work: repeatable intervals with recoverable pacing
- Cooldown: calm breathing and cyclical tissue recovery
- Progression: better repeatability and cleaner recovery

### Cardio HIIT
- Goal: high-output intervals with honest recovery
- Warm-up: accelerate safely and be ready to attack round one
- Main work: short hard efforts, clear rest, repeatable intensity
- Cooldown: breathing and lower-leg reset
- Progression: slightly tighter recovery or slightly bigger output, not both at once

### CrewFit
- Goal: high-energy mixed conditioning with repeatable momentum
- Warm-up: movement confidence and rhythm
- Main work: mixed conditioning stations that flow well
- Cooldown: keep people moving while effort comes down
- Progression: work rate and transition efficiency

### XTX
- Goal: bridge strength and conditioning smoothly
- Warm-up: prepare both loaded patterns and breathing
- Main work: resistance + conditioning with technical clarity
- Cooldown: breathing first, tissue reset second
- Progression: denser hybrid work without losing shape

### Strength Endurance
- Goal: keep force output under fatigue
- Warm-up: prepare loaded patterns and trunk stiffness
- Main work: moderate reps, repeatable force, moderate rest
- Cooldown: unload the prime movers and calm the system
- Progression: a little more density or a little more repeat volume

### Shred
- Goal: continuous mixed-modality calorie-burning work
- Warm-up: simple rhythm and low-complexity prep
- Main work: moderate resistance, repeatable output, minimal dead time
- Cooldown: gradual descent from continuous work
- Progression: higher total work and better continuity

### Balanced
- Goal: movement quality, stability, range, and broad coverage
- Warm-up: control-led prep and joint positioning
- Main work: slower, cleaner station work with moderate recovery
- Cooldown: easy recovery work and breathing reset
- Progression: better control and tolerance before more intensity

## Work Cap Philosophy
- `reps`: cap the time realistically for the rep target and movement complexity
- `hold`: cap normally matches the hold target
- `time`: time is the prescription
- `calories`: cap protects pacing and prevents one station from turning into a grind
- `distance`: cap protects pace realism
- `steps`: cap protects rhythm and station flow

## Conversion Rule
Old Ravqen sessions used generic defaults like:

- main work `170 sec`
- rest `30 sec`
- cycles `3`

Those values should be normalized through program-aware coaching rules unless the admin has clearly customized them away from the old defaults.
